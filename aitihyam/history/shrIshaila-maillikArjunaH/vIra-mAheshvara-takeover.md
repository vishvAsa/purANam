Concerning the abrupt commencement of our extant inscriptional record at the Mallikarjuna Temple at Srisailam, Parabrahma Sastry (1994) suggests that the year 1313 marked a pivotal moment in which the governing authority of the Mallikarjuna Temple complex was transferred into the hands of the "Andhra Virasaivas," as he terms them. Sastry even goes so far as to suggest that, as a characteristically violent religious community, the Virasaivas of Srisailam sought to exonerate their violent takeover of the Mallikarjuna Temple by expurgating the entire record of prior patronage on temple grounds. 

Nevertheless, other explanatory possibilities present themselves, given the political events immediately preceding this date. The years immediately before and after the dawn of the fourteenth century were characterized by significant political turmoil across much of the Deccan and south India due, in part, to the military campaigns mobilized by the Delhi Sultanate beginning with Alauddin Khalji's raid on Devgiri in Maharashtra in 1296.
